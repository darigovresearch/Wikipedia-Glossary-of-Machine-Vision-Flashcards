<b><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Histogram"" title=""Histogram"">Histogram</a></b> (Wikipedia Glossary of Machine Vision)|"In <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistics"" title=""Statistics"">statistics</a>, a <b>histogram</b> is a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphical_display"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Graphical display"">graphical display</a> of tabulated <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frequency_(statistics)"" title=""Frequency (statistics)"">frequencies</a>. A histogram is the graphical version of a table which shows what proportion of cases fall into each of several or many specified <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Categorization"" title=""Categorization"">categories</a>. The histogram differs from a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bar_chart"" title=""Bar chart"">bar chart</a> in that it is the <i>area</i> of the bar that denotes the value, not the height, a crucial distinction when the categories are not of uniform width (Lancaster, 1974). The categories are usually specified as non-overlapping <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interval_(mathematics)"" title=""Interval (mathematics)"">intervals</a> of some variable.
